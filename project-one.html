Process Site

The main goal of the process website's layout is to be a semi-lightweight mobile first responsive portfolio website that shows off my design work. The website skewed slightly more towards aesthetics than accessibility. The process site uses a shrinking navigation bar on the main page and the about page to act as a introduction to users — telling them who I am and what I do immediately when they load the page. Initially I used a blue and pink colour scheme on my process site, but I switched to a more simple colour scheme that consists of warm redish pink hues. This colour scheme allows my site to pop while still retaining a fairly simple colour pallet. My first iteration had a 2.5D layout, where some objects and sections appeared to float over other objects and sections. I kept the spirit of this 2.5D layout with my home page; where the images appear to be slightly floating above the background. The design makes the assumption that the user is aware of the visual language of the web; requiring them to know that the hamburger icon opens the menu and the arrow in the bottom right scrolls the page back to the top.





Wikipedia Article Redesign

For this portion of the project I redesigned the Wikipedia article for the Utah Tea Pot. The main goal of the Wikipedia article redesign was to be a lightweight WCAG 2.0 AA compliant responsive website while still looking aesthetically pleasing. Keeping with the general design ethos of Wikipedia, I decided to use a very minimal colour scheme consisting of two shades of blue, black, white, and gray. I used a fairly large sized Open Sans Regular — 18px on desktop — for my body text for ease of reading. The website’s word-mark is made up of two sizes of Halcyon Bold. I also added a pagination in the footer of each page, so that when the reader reaches the end of any given page they don’t need to scroll back to the top navigation.
